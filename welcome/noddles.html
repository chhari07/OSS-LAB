Lo Mein & Chow Mein

Both lo mein and chow mein are made with the same type of wheat flour and egg noodles — the difference lies in the rest of the preparation (via Taste Atlas).

Udon

A Japanese staple, udon noodles are made using wheat flour, water, and salt, and measure between 2 and 4 millimeters in width (via MasterClass)

Soba

Another Japanese classic, soba noodles are made with buckwheat (soba in Japanese) and often combined with wheat flour as well, ( via This is Japan).

Mei fun

Mei fun refers to both rice vermicelli noodles and dishes that showcase the thin strands.

Flat rice noodles

Whereas rice vermicelli are thin, delicate strands, flat rice noodles are wider and, per their name, flat.

Yi Mein

There are plenty of traditions and beliefs surrounding food, and yi mein (also yi mian, e-fu, yi fu) are considered to be longevity noodles, commonly served at Chinese New Year celebrations.
